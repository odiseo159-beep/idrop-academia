## The major BSC NFT venues

Three marketplaces handle the bulk of BSC NFT activity:

### Element Market (`element.market`)

- **History**: BSC-native marketplace launched in 2021. Multi-chain (also on ETH, Polygon, Solana via Magic Eden).
- **Volume**: consistently the highest BSC NFT volume.
- **Fee structure**: typically 2% take rate, royalties as set by collection.
- **Strengths**: Best BSC collection coverage, decent UX, native trait filtering.
- **Weaknesses**: less polished than OpenSea on Ethereum; some collections have shallow liquidity.

### OpenSea (`opensea.io`) — BSC support

- **History**: original Ethereum NFT marketplace, added BSC support in 2022.
- **Volume**: lower BSC share than Element, but the brand recognition pulls in occasional cross-chain users.
- **Fee structure**: 2.5% standard; royalties have varied (currently honors EIP-2981 on most chains).
- **Strengths**: brand trust, polished UX, trait/rarity tooling.
- **Weaknesses**: smaller BSC collection inventory; some BSC features lag the Ethereum experience.

### Magic Eden (`magiceden.io`) — multi-chain

- **History**: Solana-native marketplace, expanded to BSC and others.
- **Volume**: small on BSC compared to Element.
- **Strengths**: cross-chain UX, particularly strong if you also operate on Solana.

For BSC-specific activity, **Element is your default**. OpenSea is worth checking for specific collections it supports. Magic Eden if you're already there.

## How a typical purchase works

The mechanics are similar across marketplaces. Here's the buy flow on Element:

1. Find a collection (e.g. by browsing or via direct URL).
2. Filter by trait, price range, etc. The "Items" tab shows live listings.
3. Click an item → see its trait breakdown, price history, ownership history.
4. Click **Buy Now**.
5. MetaMask popup: review the transaction. The fee breakdown will show:
   - Item price (paid to seller)
   - Marketplace fee (paid to Element)
   - Creator royalty (paid to original creator, per EIP-2981)
   - Gas (paid to BSC validators)
6. Confirm. Within seconds, the NFT is in your wallet and the seller's wallet receives the proceeds (minus fees and royalty).

If the price is in BNB, your wallet just needs BNB plus gas. If priced in WBNB or another token, you'll need that token plus a BNB balance for gas.

## Listing your NFT for sale

The reverse flow:

1. Open the NFT in your wallet on the marketplace.
2. Click **List for sale**.
3. Choose:
   - Price (in BNB, WBNB, or USDT depending on marketplace support)
   - Listing duration (3 days, 7 days, 30 days, etc.)
   - Optional: split listing or auction (less common)
4. **First time only per collection**: you sign a `setApprovalForAll` transaction granting the marketplace permission to transfer NFTs from this collection. This is one-time per collection per marketplace.
5. Sign the listing itself — this is usually an off-chain signature, not a transaction (so it's free, no gas).

When someone buys, the marketplace contract transfers the NFT from your wallet to the buyer using the approval you granted earlier.

> **Critical**: the `setApprovalForAll` you sign is the trust point. If the marketplace contract is ever exploited, that approval lets the attacker move your NFTs. Audit your approvals at revoke.cash regularly.

## Bids vs listings: two ways to trade

A **listing** is a seller saying "I'll sell this specific token for X." Anyone can fill it instantly.

A **bid** (sometimes called an "offer") is a buyer saying "I'll buy this specific token (or any token in this collection) for X." The seller can choose to accept any bid that meets their price.

There are two flavors of bids:

- **Item-specific bid**: I'll buy *this exact token #5621* for 5 BNB.
- **Collection bid (or "trait bid")**: I'll buy *any* token in this collection (or matching specific traits) for 4 BNB. Whoever wants to sell into the bid first gets the deal.

For collectors hunting deals: collection bids are how you get the floor without paying premium for traits you don't care about.

For sellers: accepting bids gives you flexibility on timing — list, walk away, accept a bid when one shows up.

## Fees, summarized

The cumulative cost of selling an NFT is:

```
Sale price - marketplace fee - creator royalty - gas to fulfill
= net to seller
```

A typical breakdown for a 1 BNB sale on Element with 5% creator royalty:

- 1.00 BNB sale
- −0.02 BNB Element fee (2%)
- −0.05 BNB creator royalty
- −~0.0005 BNB gas
- = ~0.93 BNB to seller

For buyers, the displayed price typically excludes gas, so add ~$0.10-1 worth of BNB to whatever the listing says.

## Floor, top bid, and reading a collection

Three numbers tell you most of what you need to know about a collection's market state:

- **Floor**: lowest current listing price. The "I want one now" price.
- **Top bid**: highest standing offer. The "I want to dump now" price.
- **24h volume**: how much actually changed hands recently.

If the spread between floor and top bid is wide (e.g. floor 5 BNB, top bid 1 BNB), the collection has shallow liquidity — you can't easily exit at floor.

If 24h volume is near zero relative to listed supply, similar story: nominal floor exists but no one is buying.

For PFP-style collections, also look at:

- **Floor by trait**: rarer trait combinations have higher floors.
- **Listed ratio**: % of collection currently listed for sale. Higher = more selling pressure.

## A practical buying workflow

When you've identified a collection you want to participate in:

1. **Read the project's docs and Twitter**. Understand what you're buying conceptually.
2. **Check the contract on BscScan**. Verified? Owner renounced? Mint capped?
3. **Look at the collection page on Element**. Floor, top bid, 24h volume, total supply.
4. **Check active listings**. Filter by traits you care about. Compare prices.
5. **Look at recent sales** (last 50 transactions). Does the floor reflect what's actually transacting, or is it stale?
6. **Set a max price you're willing to pay**. Don't chase. Enter via listing or via your own bid.
7. **Buy with a fresh wallet** if you're particularly cautious about approvals.
