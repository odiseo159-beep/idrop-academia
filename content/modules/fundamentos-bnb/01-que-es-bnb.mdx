## A 60-second history

The chain we call **BNB Chain** today started life in 2017 as **Binance Coin** — a simple ERC-20 token on Ethereum used to pay fees on the Binance exchange. That was it. No chain, no smart contracts, no DeFi.

Things moved fast:

- **2019** — Binance launched its own chain, **Binance Chain**, focused on a single use case: a decentralized exchange. It was fast but had no smart contracts.
- **2020** — They launched a second, parallel chain called **Binance Smart Chain (BSC)** that *did* support smart contracts and was compatible with Ethereum tooling. Suddenly DeFi exploded on it.
- **2022** — The whole ecosystem was rebranded. "Binance Coin" became **Build N Build (BNB)**. The two chains became **BNB Beacon Chain** (the original) and **BNB Smart Chain** (the EVM one). The umbrella name is now **BNB Chain**.
- **2024** — The original Beacon Chain was officially sunset. Staking and governance moved on-chain to BSC. The ecosystem also added **opBNB** (an L2 rollup) and **BNB Greenfield** (decentralized storage).

So when somebody says "BNB Chain" today without further context, they almost always mean **BNB Smart Chain**.

## What does it actually do?

BNB Smart Chain is a **general-purpose EVM-compatible blockchain**. Two phrases there matter:

**General-purpose**: anyone can deploy a smart contract to it. There's a DEX (PancakeSwap), there are lending protocols (Venus, Kinza), there are NFT marketplaces, there are games, there are memecoins. The chain doesn't care what you build.

**EVM-compatible**: it speaks the same language as Ethereum. The same Solidity code, the same wallets (MetaMask, Rabby, etc.), the same standards (ERC-20 becomes BEP-20, ERC-721 becomes BEP-721). If you've used Ethereum, the mental model transfers almost 1:1.

The two big differences with Ethereum are **fees** and **speed**: BSC transactions typically cost a few cents and confirm in around a second. That's the trade-off the chain makes — fewer validators (more on that next lesson) in exchange for cheaper, faster blocks.

## Why this matters for the rest of the academy

Almost everything you'll do on BNB Chain in this course — connecting a wallet, swapping a token, reading a contract on BscScan, evaluating a memecoin — happens on **BSC specifically**. opBNB and Greenfield are interesting but not where you start.

Keep this map in your head:

> **BNB** = the token
> **BNB Chain** = the ecosystem
> **BNB Smart Chain (BSC)** = the EVM chain you'll use 95% of the time
> **opBNB** = a Layer 2 rollup on top of BSC, even cheaper
> **BNB Greenfield** = a separate chain for decentralized storage

If you remember just that, you're ahead of most people writing about it on Twitter.
