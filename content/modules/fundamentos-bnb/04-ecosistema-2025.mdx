## Three chains, one ecosystem

The BNB Chain ecosystem in 2025 has three live members. They are *separate chains*, but they're designed to work together:

| Chain | Role | EVM? | When you use it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **BNB Smart Chain (BSC)** | General-purpose execution | Yes | 95% of the time |
| **opBNB** | Optimistic Rollup L2 | Yes | High-throughput apps, gaming, microtransactions |
| **BNB Greenfield** | Decentralized storage | No (own VM) | Storing data with onchain permissions |

Most users only interact with BSC. opBNB and Greenfield are infrastructure layers that specific apps build on.

## opBNB in one paragraph

If you've heard of Optimism or Arbitrum on Ethereum, opBNB is the same idea applied to BSC. Transactions are executed off the main chain in a separate environment, then *batched* and posted back to BSC for security. The result: fees in fractions of a cent, throughput in the thousands of TPS, while inheriting BSC's security through fraud proofs.

You bridge assets from BSC to opBNB to use it. The most common use cases are **gaming** (where you need many cheap transactions per minute) and **high-frequency trading** primitives.

You probably won't start on opBNB. You'll start on BSC.

## Greenfield in one paragraph

Greenfield isn't an EVM chain — it's a purpose-built chain for **storing files** and managing **access permissions** for them. Think of it as decentralized S3 where the policy ("who can read this object") lives onchain. It's used by some apps to store NFT metadata, user data, or anything that needs durable storage with auditable access control. You won't interact with it directly as an end user; apps abstract it away.

## The dApps you'll actually use

The chain matters less than the apps running on it. A few you should know by name even before you've used them:

**PancakeSwap** — the dominant DEX. Spot swaps, liquidity pools, perpetuals, lottery, pre-launch token sales (IFOs). When someone says "I bought a token on BSC", they almost always swapped it on PancakeSwap or one of its forks.

**Venus** — the largest lending market on BSC. Deposit BNB, USDT, BTCB, etc. as collateral, borrow against them. Same model as Aave on Ethereum.

**Lista DAO** — liquid staking + a stablecoin (lisUSD) backed by collateral. Important if you want yield on your idle BNB.

**Four.meme** — a memecoin launchpad that uses bonding curves. We'll dedicate a whole module to how this category works.

**BscScan** — not a dApp but indispensable. The block explorer. Every contract address, every transaction, every token transfer is visible there. You'll have it open constantly.

**Element / OpenSea (BSC)** — NFT marketplaces. Element is the BSC-native one with the most volume.

This isn't a comprehensive list. It's the **vocabulary** you need to read most BNB-related content without getting lost.
