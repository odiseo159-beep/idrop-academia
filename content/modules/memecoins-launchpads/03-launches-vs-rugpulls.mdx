## The core question

You see a brand-new token on Four.meme or freshly added to PancakeSwap. The price is moving. Telegram is buzzing. The question that determines everything: **is this a real attempt at a community token, or a planned exit scam?**

Both look superficially similar in the first hours. The differences are mechanical, observable on-chain, and learnable in 10 minutes.

## The taxonomy of rugs

Not every failed launch is a rug. Distinguishing categories matters:

### Liquidity rug (LP rug)

The team is the only LP. After enough buyers fill the pool with BNB, they call `removeLiquidity`. The BNB leaves with them, the pool drops to zero BNB, and your tokens become unsellable.

**Detection**: check who owns the LP tokens.
- LP tokens held by the team wallet → high rug risk.
- LP tokens held by `0x000...dead` (burn address) → permanent.
- LP tokens held by a known locker contract (PinkLock, UNCX, Team.Finance) → locked for the lock duration. Verify the duration.

### Mint rug (supply inflation)

The contract has a `mint()` function callable by the owner. After buyers accumulate, the owner mints a massive new supply, dumps it into the pool, and exits with the BNB. Holders' tokens are diluted to ~0.

**Detection**: read the contract on BscScan.
- Look for any `mint`, `_mint`, `setSupply`, `inflate` functions.
- Check if they're `onlyOwner` and whether ownership is renounced.
- Capped supply tokens (no public mint, immutable cap) can't be mint-rugged.

### Honeypot

Buy works. Sell reverts. Common implementations:

- Blacklist: contract maintains a blacklist; non-whitelisted addresses can't transfer.
- Sell tax of 99%: technically you can sell, but you receive 1% of expected value.
- Transfer modifier with hidden conditions: looks like a normal transfer but reverts based on caller logic.

**Detection**:
- **Token Sniffer** (`tokensniffer.com`) and **Honeypot.is** programmatically test buy/sell behavior. Free, fast, accurate.
- Read the contract for any custom transfer logic. Standard OpenZeppelin ERC20 = clean. Modified `_transfer` overrides = read carefully.
- Test with a tiny amount first: buy 0.001 BNB worth, then immediately try to sell. If sell fails, it's a honeypot.

### Slow rug / soft rug

The team gradually sells their allocation over weeks or months. Price slowly bleeds while social channels insist "we're building". By the time it's obvious, holders are 80% down.

**Detection**: monitor known team wallets via BscScan. Sustained outflows to exchanges = distribution.

### Tax rug

The contract sets very high buy/sell taxes (e.g. 50% sell tax). Taxes go to a "marketing wallet" controlled by the team. Effectively a slow drain of every transaction.

Tokens with high taxes can be legitimate (taxes can buy back, fund development, lock more LP). But high taxes + opaque tax flow + team-controlled tax wallet = rug structure.

**Detection**: read the contract for `_taxFee`, `_liquidityFee`, `_marketingFee`. Trace where the proceeds go. If they go to an EOA you don't trust, treat as rug-adjacent.

## What a "fair" launch looks like, mechanically

A launch designed not to rug has these properties — verifiable on-chain:

**1. Liquidity locked or burned**

- LP tokens sent to `0x000...dead` (burned) or to a known time-locked contract.
- Lock duration ≥ 6 months, ideally 1 year+ for a project that wants to demonstrate commitment.

**2. Ownership renounced**

- `owner()` returns `0x000...0000`.
- After this, no one can call `onlyOwner` functions. No new mints, no tax changes, no blacklist additions, no upgrades.

**3. No malicious functions in the contract**

- Standard BEP-20 functions only, or extensions that have been audited and explained.
- No hidden mint, no blacklist, no transfer pause.

**4. Reasonable token distribution**

- Top holder isn't 50%+ of supply (excluding the LP itself and burn address).
- Multiple early buyers, not just a handful of bot-funded wallets.

**5. Verifiable team commitment (optional, depends on project)**

- For non-anon teams: doxxed founders with reputational stake.
- Multi-sig contract owner before renouncement.
- Public roadmap with on-chain milestones.

For pure memecoins, point 5 is often skipped — the value proposition doesn't require team credibility once 1-4 are in place. The contract is the team after renouncement.

## Reading the BscScan signals

Walking through a fresh launch on BscScan, in order:

1. **Token page header**: Is the contract verified? (Required.)
2. **"Holders" tab**:
   - Is there a `0x000...dead` entry holding the LP tokens? Good sign.
   - Or is the LP held by a locker contract? Click through, verify lock duration.
   - Or is the LP held by an EOA (and address starting with non-trivial hash)? Risk.
3. **"Read Contract" tab**:
   - `owner()` returns `0x000...0000`? Good.
   - `totalSupply()` matches what's claimed?
4. **"Write Contract" tab** (if not renounced):
   - What functions are `onlyOwner`?
   - Pay particular attention to `setTaxFee`, `_mint`, `pause`, `addToBlacklist`.
5. **"Source Code" tab**:
   - If you can read Solidity even a bit, scan for unusual patterns.
   - Look at the constructor and the `_transfer` function.
   - Or paste into Token Sniffer for an automated read.

In ~3 minutes you can do this triage for any token. Make it a habit before any buy.

## Locker tools you'll see

The "LP locked at X" claim is verifiable. The major lockers on BSC:

- **PinkLock** (`pink.bsc/lock`): widely used. Lock can be checked at `pink.bsc/lock/[lock_id]`.
- **UNCX Network** (`uncx.network`): cross-chain LP locker.
- **Team Finance** (`team.finance/view-coin/[address]`): also offers vesting for team tokens.

Each shows: amount locked, unlock date, owner. Verify these match what the team claims. "Liquidity locked for 100 years!" should be checkable in seconds.

## A 60-second rug check script

For any token someone shows you:

1. Open BscScan. Contract verified? (No → walk away.)
2. Holders tab. LP locked / burned? (No → walk away.)
3. Owner renounced? (Or multisig with credible signers?)
4. Token Sniffer or Honeypot.is. Honeypot detected? (Yes → walk away.)
5. Top holder concentration. Top 10 < 50%? (No → high risk.)
6. Contract creator history. Repeat-deployer? (Yes → high risk.)

If all six pass, you've eliminated most overt rug risk. Remaining risk is market risk (price action, attention dynamics) — the legitimate uncertainty.

## What you can't detect

Some risks are nearly invisible until they trigger:

- **Future contract upgrades** if proxy pattern is used and ownership not renounced.
- **Governance attacks** if the project has a DAO with low voter turnout.
- **Off-chain agreements** between team and CEX that we never see.
- **Insider information** about exit timing.

For these, time is your best friend. A launch that's been around for months without incident is more trustworthy than yesterday's launch with all the right boxes checked.
