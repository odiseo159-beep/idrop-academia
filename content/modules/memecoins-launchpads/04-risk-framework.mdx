## The framework

Memecoin participation is a probability game with extreme outcomes. Your job is to set up the rules so that:

- Most positions go to zero (expected, fine).
- Occasional positions go up 5-50x (where your returns come from).
- No single position can knock you out (the survival constraint).

This requires three things:

1. **Position sizing** that limits any single loss.
2. **Pre-committed exit rules** that capture upside without requiring discipline in the moment.
3. **Portfolio-level cap** on memecoin exposure so the speculation doesn't consume everything else.

## Position sizing

For any individual memecoin trade, ask: **what's the maximum I'd be willing to lose entirely?**

A common framework:

- **Total portfolio**: your entire crypto + savings + everything you'd consider "long-term capital".
- **Speculation budget**: 1-5% of total portfolio. The amount you allocate to risky bets (memes, low-cap alts, leveraged positions). Lose all of it = doesn't change your life.
- **Per-trade size**: 5-20% of speculation budget. So if you have a 5% speculation budget and ~10% per-trade rule, each trade is 0.5% of your total portfolio.

Math: if 9 of 10 trades go to zero and 1 goes 20x, your speculation budget is roughly flat. If 9 go to zero and 1 goes 50x, you're up 5x on the budget. If all 10 go to zero (very possible), your total portfolio is down 5%. Survivable.

The mistake most newcomers make: putting 30-50% of total capital into a single "high conviction" memecoin. When it goes to zero (and it usually does), they're financially set back years.

## Pre-committed exit ladders

You should know how you'll exit before you enter. Writing it down (literally, in a notes app) commits you in advance.

A simple example for a memecoin position:

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| Position drops 50% from entry | Exit fully. (Stop-loss.) |
| Position is up 2x from entry | Sell 25% (now risk-free on the original capital). |
| Position is up 5x from entry | Sell another 25%. |
| Position is up 10x from entry | Sell another 25%. |
| Position is up 25x+ | Sell another 12.5%. Hold remainder as moonbag. |
| Influencer chatter dries up | Sell aggressively regardless of price level. |
| Liquidity drops > 30% | Exit — graduation reversal in progress. |

Write your own. The specific numbers matter less than the act of pre-committing.

The discipline is psychological. When a position is up 5x, you'll *feel* like it's going to 50x. Every dollar you sell at 5x feels like leaving money on the table. Selling anyway, on schedule, is what separates consistent traders from the ones who give all the gains back.

## Portfolio caps

Even with good per-trade rules, you can blow up if you have too many concurrent positions. Limit:

- **Total memecoin allocation**: never above 10% of total portfolio at any time.
- **Concurrent memecoin positions**: 3-5 max. Every additional position is more attention demanded.
- **Frequency**: don't enter a new memecoin within 24 hours of exiting one. Forces a cool-down period.

These rules exist because each memecoin trade requires real attention. If you have 20 concurrent positions, you can't watch them properly. You'll miss exit signals. You'll get rugged on at least one.

## Reading the attention cycle

Memecoin price is a function of social attention. The attention cycle has a recognizable shape:

**Phase 1 — Discovery**: a small group of early holders accumulates. Price low, volume low. Hard to find unless you're in early communities.

**Phase 2 — Initial pump**: someone with audience tweets about it. Price 5-20x. Real volume arrives. You hear about it for the first time.

**Phase 3 — Mainstream amplification**: large influencers, news bots, crypto media pick it up. Price continues climbing 2-10x further. Maximum FOMO. *This is when most retail buys.*

**Phase 4 — Distribution**: insiders and large early holders sell into retail demand. Price action becomes choppy. Volume stays high but price doesn't keep up. Twitter sentiment is still bullish.

**Phase 5 — Exhaustion**: influencers go quiet (they've already sold). Volume drops. Price drifts down. Holders rationalize. Price drops more.

**Phase 6 — Capitulation**: support breaks. Price falls 50-90% from highs. The "bagholder" community on Twitter forms. Some tokens recover (rare); most don't.

If you're entering in Phase 2 with strict exits, you can profit. If you're entering in Phase 4, you're providing exit liquidity for early holders. Most retail entries happen in Phase 4.

The skill is recognizing which phase you're in *before* the next one starts.

## The graduation trap

Specific to bonding-curve launches like Four.meme:

When a token is approaching graduation (90%+ to threshold), there's often an "everyone wants to be holding at graduation" rush that pushes price up the curve. After graduation, the flip to PancakeSwap typically triggers profit-taking. Price often drops 30-50% in the first hours post-graduation.

Two strategies:

- **Sell into the pre-graduation pump**: lock in gains, accept that graduation might run higher without you.
- **Hold through graduation**: bet that this specific token has staying power beyond the graduation event.

Both are valid. Predetermined choice = good. Improvising in the moment = bad.

## Tax considerations (briefly)

Frequent trading creates many taxable events. In most jurisdictions, every swap is a taxable disposal. Memecoin traders generating hundreds of trades per year often discover at year-end that they owe tax on positions that have since gone to zero.

- Track every trade with timestamps and BNB-denominated values.
- Consider tools like Koinly, CoinTracker, or Accointing for BSC support.
- Set aside 20-40% of realized profits for taxes throughout the year, depending on your jurisdiction.

This is operational hygiene that doesn't show up in the trading itself but matters tremendously in retrospect.

## Operational hygiene

Specific to the rough-and-tumble world of memecoin trading:

- **Use a dedicated wallet** for memecoin trades. Never connect your main savings wallet.
- **Approve only what you're trading** — never infinite approval on tokens you don't trust long-term.
- **Read the BscScan contract before buying**, even briefly. Even 30 seconds catches obvious scams.
- **Verify contract addresses** from multiple sources. The "official Twitter" account might be hijacked.
- **Don't click links from DMs or random Telegram messages**. The contract address you need is on the project's verified channels only.

These rules sound paranoid. They are. The memecoin space is the most adversarial corner of crypto, and the cost of being relaxed is total loss.

## What success looks like

Realistically, a disciplined memecoin participant over a year might:

- Make 50-100 trades.
- Have 60-80% of those go to zero or near-zero.
- Have 5-15% modest winners (1.5-3x).
- Have 1-3 large winners (5-50x).
- Net up 50-200% on speculation budget in a good year.

In a bad year (or a bear market), they're down 30-70% on the speculation budget. That's why the speculation budget exists — bad years can't take out the rest of your capital.

This is not get-rich-quick. It's a high-variance speculation strategy that compounds slowly when done right and catastrophically when done wrong.
