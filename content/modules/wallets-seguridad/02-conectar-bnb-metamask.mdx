## Step 1 — Install MetaMask cleanly

Go to **metamask.io** directly. Type the URL yourself. Do not Google "MetaMask download" and click the first ad — those ads have been used to distribute fake MetaMask installers more than once.

Install the browser extension (or the mobile app, but for learning, browser is easier).

When MetaMask launches:

1. Choose **Create a new wallet**.
2. Pick a strong password — this protects the local installation only, not the chain. If someone gets your seed phrase your password doesn't matter.
3. MetaMask will reveal your **12-word secret recovery phrase**. **Write it down on paper.** Not in a screenshot. Not in a password manager just yet. Not in your Notes app. On paper.
4. Confirm the phrase by re-entering the words in order. Done.

You now have an EOA. Default network: Ethereum Mainnet. We need to add BSC.

## Step 2 — Add BNB Smart Chain

The simplest path is via **chainlist.org**:

1. Go to `chainlist.org`.
2. Search "BNB Smart Chain Mainnet".
3. Confirm it shows **chain ID 56**.
4. Click **Connect Wallet**, then **Add to MetaMask**.
5. Approve the popup.

If you prefer to enter manually, the parameters are:

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Network name | BNB Smart Chain |
| RPC URL | `https://bsc-dataseed.bnbchain.org` |
| Chain ID | `56` |
| Currency symbol | BNB |
| Block explorer | `https://bscscan.com` |

Switch to it from the network dropdown at the top of MetaMask. Your balance will now read `0 BNB`.

> **Why chain ID 56 matters**: every signed transaction includes the chain ID. A transaction you sign for chain 56 will not execute on chain 1 (Ethereum). This prevents *replay attacks* — someone capturing your signed transaction and trying to broadcast it on another chain.

## Step 3 — Get some BNB

Your address is empty. To send any transaction (even just to receive a token transfer is free, but to *do* anything) you need BNB for gas.

Two paths, depending on your situation:

**Mainnet (real money):**

- Buy BNB on a centralized exchange (Binance, Coinbase, Kraken, etc.).
- Withdraw to your MetaMask address. **Crucially: select "BNB Smart Chain (BEP-20)" as the withdrawal network**, not "BNB Beacon Chain (BEP-2)" — that's the deprecated chain and you'll lose your funds. Not all exchanges still support BEP-2 withdrawals; many will block it.
- A typical first deposit of ~$10 in BNB is plenty for many transactions while you learn.

**Testnet (free, no real value):**

- Add the **BSC Testnet** to MetaMask (chain ID 97, search chainlist).
- Visit a faucet like `testnet.bnbchain.org/faucet-smart` and request 0.5 testnet BNB.
- This BNB has no real value and can't be sold, but it's perfect for practicing.

**For the rest of this module, testnet is fine.** You don't need to risk real money to learn the mechanics.

## Step 4 — Send your first transaction

Pick any address you control (or a friend's). In MetaMask:

1. Click **Send**.
2. Paste the recipient address. **Always verify the first 4 and last 4 characters by eye** — clipboard hijacker malware is a real category.
3. Enter an amount, e.g. `0.001 BNB`.
4. Look at the gas estimate — should be a tiny fraction of a cent.
5. Click **Confirm**.

A transaction hash appears. Click it to open BscScan. You'll see:

- The hash
- From / To addresses
- Value transferred
- Gas used and gas price
- Block number and confirmations

Congratulations. You just performed an onchain transaction without anyone's permission.

## What just happened, technically

Behind the scenes, MetaMask:

1. Built a transaction object: `{from, to, value, nonce, gasPrice, gasLimit, chainId: 56}`.
2. Asked your private key to sign it (this happened locally — your private key never left the extension).
3. Sent the signed transaction over JSON-RPC to a BSC node.
4. The node propagated it to the validator network.
5. A validator included it in a block within ~1 second.
6. The block was finalized and BscScan picked it up.

You didn't trust MetaMask with your money — you trusted it to format and broadcast a message that you signed. There's a meaningful difference.
