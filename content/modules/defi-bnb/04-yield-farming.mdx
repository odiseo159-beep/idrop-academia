## The farming flow, end to end

Yield farming is just the composition of two things you've already learned:

1. Provide liquidity → get LP tokens
2. Stake LP tokens in a farm → get additional emissions

Here's the canonical flow on PancakeSwap:

```
Hold CAKE + BNB
   ↓ deposit into CAKE/BNB pool
Receive CAKE-BNB LP tokens
   ↓ stake into CAKE/BNB farm
Earn CAKE emissions on top of swap fees
   ↓ harvest periodically
Sell CAKE / re-stake CAKE / fold back into LP
```

That last step is where compounding comes in. If you re-deposit harvested CAKE into the LP and re-stake, your position grows over time without you adding new capital. That's the basis of "auto-compounding vaults" — protocols that do the harvest+restake loop automatically.

## Where do the emissions come from?

The CAKE you earn from the farm isn't generated by trading volume. It's **minted** as part of the protocol's tokenomics — newly created tokens, distributed according to a schedule.

Each farm has a "multiplier" or "weight" that determines what share of total emissions it gets. PancakeSwap allocates X CAKE per block to all farms combined; the CAKE/BNB farm might get 30% of that, USDT/BNB might get 20%, smaller farms get smaller shares.

This means:
- **Total emissions are fixed by the protocol**.
- **Your share of any farm's emissions = your % of the staked LP supply in that farm**.
- **The dollar value of those emissions depends on the price of the reward token**.

Which leads to the most important warning in farming…

## APR is a lie (kind of)

A farm advertising "1,200% APR" isn't lying about the math. If you deposit $1,000, you might genuinely earn the equivalent of $12,000 worth of TOKEN at *current* prices over a year.

But "current prices" is the trap. Token emissions are sell pressure. As more TOKEN is minted into the market, holders sell, price drops, and the dollar value of *future* emissions falls.

For new farms with high emissions, it's common to see:

| Day | Token price | APR (in token) | APR (in USD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | $1.00 | 1,200% | 1,200% |
| 30 | $0.40 | 1,200% | 480% |
| 90 | $0.10 | 1,200% | 120% |
| 180 | $0.04 | 1,200% | 48% |

The headline APR stays the same (you still earn the same number of tokens per dollar deposited), but your dollar yield collapses as the token price drops.

A useful sanity check: imagine the price of the reward token went to **zero** in 6 months. Would you still want to be in this farm?

Answer it honestly. The answer for most "5000% APR mooncoin farms" is no.

## Sustainable vs unsustainable yield

Two extremes to anchor the spectrum:

**Sustainable**:
- USDT/USDC stablecoin LP on a major DEX, earning 5% APR from pure swap fees. No emissions, low risk, low return.
- ETH/USDC v3 LP with active range management on Ethereum, earning 15-30% from fees on real volume.

**Unsustainable**:
- CAKE/BUSD LP earning 200% APR, where 195% is CAKE emissions (sell pressure) and 5% is real fees.
- Brand new farm of "MOON/BNB" earning 50,000% APR for the first week, then the team rugs.

The lower-yield options compound. The higher-yield ones are extracting value from someone (often the next round of farmers).

## The CAKE Syrup Pool model

PancakeSwap also has **Syrup Pools** — single-asset staking. Stake CAKE alone, earn either more CAKE (the standard pool) or partner project tokens (IFO and incentive pools).

This is simpler than farming because there's no LP token, no impermanent loss. But it ties up your CAKE, and the rewards quality varies wildly. The classic CAKE-for-CAKE pool has been a reliable single-digit-APR option for years.

## Auto-compounders and vaults

Manually harvesting and re-investing is annoying. Protocols like **Beefy Finance** and PancakeSwap's own auto-CAKE pool automate it: they harvest your rewards every few hours, sell or re-LP automatically, and restake.

Pros: better APY (compounding), less gas spent on manual harvest, less mental overhead.

Cons: another smart contract to trust, additional fee taken by the vault (usually 1-5% of yield), and your position is now exposed to two protocols' risk instead of one.

For positions you're going to leave for weeks or months, vaults usually win on net. For positions you're actively managing, manual is fine.

## Practical decision framework

Before depositing into any farm, answer:

1. **Is the underlying pool one I'd be in for fees alone?** If you wouldn't LP this pair without emissions, the emissions are bribing you to take a bad position.
2. **What % of the APR is real (fees) vs emissions?** Read the protocol docs. If they don't break it down, that's a flag.
3. **What's the reward token's emission schedule?** Halving every 3 months? Constant forever? The schedule tells you whether yield will hold or collapse.
4. **Can I withdraw at any time?** Most farms yes; some have lockups. Read before staking.
5. **How long until breakeven on gas?** Small positions in expensive networks can take weeks of yield just to break even on the deposit gas. (Less of an issue on BSC, but still worth checking.)
