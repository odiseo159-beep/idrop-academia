## The pitch in one paragraph

**DeFi** is a way to do the things banks do — saving, borrowing, swapping, betting — without the bank. Instead of a corporation holding your money and deciding whether to let you use it, smart contracts hold the funds and execute the rules transparently. You connect a wallet, you sign a transaction, the contract does what it's coded to do. There's no signup, no manager, no business hours.

That's the elevator pitch. Now let's unpack what it actually means in practice.

## The four basic primitives

Almost every DeFi protocol you'll encounter is some combination of these four building blocks:

**Swapping**: trade one token for another instantly. The biggest example on BSC is **PancakeSwap**. The mechanism behind it (an Automated Market Maker, or AMM) is the next lesson — but the user experience is: pick token A, pick token B, click swap.

**Lending and borrowing**: deposit a token to earn interest, or use deposits as collateral to borrow other tokens. **Venus** is the canonical example on BSC. You deposit BNB, the protocol gives you a fixed % yield. Or you deposit BNB and borrow USDT against it.

**Liquidity provision**: put two tokens into a pool that other people use to swap, and earn a share of the swap fees. This is how AMMs work under the hood — and how a lot of DeFi yield gets generated.

**Yield farming**: combine the above. Provide liquidity, receive LP tokens, stake those LP tokens in a "farm" to earn additional rewards (often the protocol's governance token). Sometimes called "money legos" because each layer composes on top of the previous one.

There's a whole zoo of more exotic primitives — perpetual futures, options, prediction markets, real-world asset tokenization — but if you understand the four above, you can read most DeFi protocols.

## Why BNB Chain specifically

Ethereum was the original home of DeFi. It still has the deepest liquidity and the most innovation. But for most of 2020-2021, doing a $50 swap on Ethereum cost $30-100 in gas. That priced out an entire generation of retail users.

BNB Smart Chain offered the same Solidity-based smart contract platform with $0.10 swap fees. The result was an explosion: **PancakeSwap** became the biggest DEX by trade count in the world, **Venus** built up billions in lending TVL, and a long tail of yield farms and forks ran a fast-and-furious 2021 cycle.

The cost of that accessibility was honest:

- **More scams**. Lower deployment cost = more rug pulls. Always check audits and team reputation.
- **More forks**. Many protocols on BSC are direct copies of Ethereum protocols (PancakeSwap is largely a fork of Uniswap v2/v3, Venus of Compound). That's not bad — proven mechanisms — but it means innovation often happens on Ethereum first.
- **More volatility in the long-tail**. The smaller a token's market cap, the more violently it moves.

Today BSC is one of the top 5 chains by DeFi TVL, alongside Ethereum, Solana, Tron and a few L2s.

## What you actually need to start

To do anything in this module hands-on, you need:

1. A wallet (covered in the previous module — MetaMask on BSC is fine).
2. A few dollars of BNB for gas.
3. Optional: some stablecoins (USDT or USDC on BSC) to actually swap or lend with. You can skip this and stick to testnet for the mechanics.

If you've completed the **Wallets & Security** module, you're set. If not, do that first — the hard part of DeFi is not understanding it, it's not getting drained while you learn.
